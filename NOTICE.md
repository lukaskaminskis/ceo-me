## Primary source — Matt Mochary

The overwhelming majority of frameworks, scripts, and language in
`SKILL.md` originate from Matt Mochary's publicly published works.

- **Mochary Method Curriculum** (master document, publicly shared)
  https://docs.google.com/document/d/18FiJbYn53fTtPmphfdCKT2TMWH-8Y2L-MLqDk-MFV4s/

- **The Great CEO Within** (book, Lioncrest Publishing, 2019)
  https://smile.amazon.com/Great-CEO-Within-Tactical-Building/dp/0578599287

- **Mochary Method** (coaching practice, coaches, programs)
  https://www.mocharymethod.com

Frameworks attributed to Matt Mochary include but are not limited to:
Top Goal, On Time, First 2 Minutes, Energy Audit, Zone of Genius, Heard,
Difficult Conversations (8 steps), 5 A's of Receiving Feedback, Giving
Feedback (3 levels), Praise, Magic Questions, DRIs, AORs, Issue/Proposed
Solution Template, Clean Escalate, Agreement Tracker, Process Audit,
Three Strikes Escalation, 1-1 Template, OKR Creation, Anti-Sell,
Backchannels/Top-Grading, Firing Well, Trust + Like, Trust Deep, Mission
/ Vision / Values, Board Best Practice, Fundraising Relationship Method,
Triangulation, Make Money / Have Fun / Do Good, CEO as Fireman, the CEO
Team structure, and the Mochary Method Curriculum's organizing taxonomy
of the CEO role.

**"Mochary Method"** and **"The Great CEO Within"** are marks of Matt
Mochary, used here nominatively to identify the source.

## Other framework authors referenced

The Mochary Method Curriculum itself references and synthesizes the work
of many other authors. Where those frameworks appear in `SKILL.md`,
attribution is owed to:

### Conscious Leadership Group
**Jim Dethmer, Diana Chapman, Kaley Warner Klemp**
- *The 15 Commitments of Conscious Leadership* (2014)
- The "above the line / below the line" model
- The Drama Triangle (Villain / Victim / Hero) and the responsibility
  shift framework
- https://conscious.is

### Byron Katie
- **The Work** — the 4 questions and the turnaround.
- *Loving What Is* (2002) and subsequent works.
- https://thework.com

### L. David Marquet (U.S. Navy, retired)
- **"I Intend To…"** — from *Turn the Ship Around!* (Penguin, 2013)
- https://intent-basedleadership.com

### David Allen
- **GTD (Getting Things Done)** — the capture / clarify / organize /
  reflect / engage methodology, the Next Action / Waiting For /
  Someday-Maybe / Reference list structure.
- *Getting Things Done* (Penguin, 2001/2015).

### Greg McKeown
- **Top Goal** as referenced by Mochary derives from McKeown's notion of
  scheduling time for the highest-priority work in *Essentialism* (Crown
  Business, 2014).

### Jeff Bezos / Amazon
- **Type 1 vs Type 2 Decisions** (one-way doors vs two-way doors) — from
  Bezos's 2015 shareholder letter.
- **The 6-Page Memo** — Amazon's narrative-memo practice.
- "Disagree and Commit" as articulated in Amazon's leadership principles.

### David Rock (NeuroLeadership Institute)
- **SCARF model** (Status, Certainty, Autonomy, Relatedness, Fairness).
- *Your Brain at Work* (HarperBusiness, 2009) and related publications.

### April Dunford
- **Product Positioning — the 5-component framework.**
- *Obviously Awesome* (Ambient Press, 2019).

### Bill Aulet
- **Beachhead market** strategy.
- *Disciplined Entrepreneurship* (Wiley, 2013).

### Bill Campbell
- The **Performance Review** structure (Results / Management / Innovation
  / Peer Collaboration) as documented by Mochary derives from Bill
  Campbell's coaching practice with Silicon Valley CEOs.

### Frank Slootman
- **"Bias to Action"** principle as referenced.
- *Amp It Up* (Wiley, 2022).

### Dave Kashen
- **Conscious Listening** framework (Head / Heart / Gut formula),
  developed within the Conscious Leadership Group ecosystem.

### Sam Altman
- Sleep / Exercise / Nutrition protocol as published on his personal
  blog (samaltman.com) and shared through the Mochary curriculum.

### Alex MacCaw
- Health protocols as shared through Clearbit and the Mochary
  curriculum.

### Hendricks Institute
- **Zone of Genius** concept — Gay Hendricks, *The Big Leap*
  (HarperOne, 2009).

### Susan Scott
- **Difficult Conversations** as a discipline — *Fierce Conversations*
  (Berkley, 2002).

### Stephen Karpman, MD
- **The Drama Triangle** original psychological model (1968), as later
  extended by the Conscious Leadership Group.
